Somethingyou (SY) — Structure, Layers, and Relationship with OKTRIA

This document covers the structure, layers, commercial relationship, and what sits where between SY and OKTRIA — landing the plane across each dimension.

1. Simplified — What SY Is and What It Does for OKTRIA

The purpose of SY is not to invent products. It is to let others — in this case OKTRIA — See it. Structure it. Move it. Scale it.

SY does this by providing:

  • Ecosystem orchestration in the areas known to be the issue — multi-party workflows, settlement, reconciliation, participant lifecycle, alerting. Plus the logic on primes, contracting and legals, and the ontology on who pays whom from what bucket and how every participant sees it from their perspective
  • Local models for each operator — with ingress and egress, self-configuration, and the ability to improve models over time so the product becomes more effective with every transaction
  • Ledgering — the financial truth layer that makes commercial terms executable and auditable across all participants

SY deploys where it sees the value proposition as high — so that it can receive a percentage of the value created. SY only earns when OKTRIA earns. COGS line, 5–10% of revenue, aligned by design.

Important: SY is not in the payment flow. It does not process transactions and it does not store data. SY is ecosystem management, integration, workflow orchestration, and the ETL data pipeline that feeds the model it gives operators to drive their product. Money moves through the existing payment infrastructure (DMG, Fiserv, acquirers). SY sits alongside — orchestrating, not transacting.

OKTRIA owns the product, the customer, the brand, and every commercial decision. SY is the engine underneath — invisible to the operator, visible on the P&L as infrastructure cost.

Clients never see SY. It is always behind the ecosystem participant — in this case, OKTRIA. The operator sees OKTRIA. The venue staff see OKTRIA. SY is invisible.

And to be clear: if OKTRIA wants to build any of these components itself, it is welcome to do so — and SY would not be needed for those parts. What we find is that in OKTRIA's case, focusing on the product value is best. Leave the plumbing to SY. Leave the processing to the payment value chain (DMG, Fiserv, acquirers). OKTRIA's energy goes where OKTRIA's value is — the product, the operator relationship, and the commercial proposition.

2. Role across the three layers

LayerWhat SY Provides
Intelligence Knowledge substrate that grounds decisions in verified truth. Reconciles conflicting inputs before they reach decision-makers. Extends proven models into new territory. Audits provenance.
Orchestration Mobius maps the ecosystem, renders participation, runs settlement, reconciliation, fee distribution, and dynamic pricing. Carries the shift from price-taker to price-maker that unsticks commercial conversations.
Delivery Two modes. Oloid: A2A agent deployed locally into a participant's workspace — participates in their tools, doesn't replace them. Mobieye: hosted engagement surface for participants without integration. Every participant gets their own model. Meet them where they are.

OKTRIA sits on top of all three. Payments is one touchpoint in OKTRIA's ecosystem, not the subject of it.

3. OKTRIA ↔ SY — commercially and operationally

SY is the service provider. OKTRIA is the client.

Operationally: SY provides platform, intelligence, delivery surfaces, and pricing engine. OKTRIA provides the ecosystem, participants, commercial model, market, and mandate.

Commercially: SY is a COGS line item for OKTRIA — the same way a payment processor sits inside the cost of revenue, not as a separate SaaS invoice. OKTRIA pays SY a percentage of the revenue generated when utilising the capability — in the range of 5–10%, depending on utilisation. Clean, scalable, and aligned: SY only earns when OKTRIA earns.

The commercial principle is set: SY stays upstream as infrastructure cost, OKTRIA stays downstream as the product and customer relationship.
